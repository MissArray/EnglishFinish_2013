  Training in academic writing for non-native English speakers

English Finish now offers bespoke intensive workshops on academic writing skills for non-native English speakers – at your university.


Who can benefit?

Academics, postdocs, PhD students who have to or aim to publish their research in English but are non-native English speakers; academics who already publish in English and aim for top-tier journals.


What do the workshops offer?
  • Various modules are available, combining group sessions with one-to-one coaching. 
  • Intensive tutorial-only modules are also available.
  • Each workshop lasts between 1 and 5 days, depending on the number of participants and the requirements of the department that commissions it.

 
Overall aims:

  • Enhance your ability to write clearly for academic purposes (e.g. research papers, monographs, conference submissions, proposals for funding).
  • Boost your chances of getting a paper accepted for publication in a top-tier journal.
  • Increase your chances of participating in international research programmes.
  • Collaborate more effectively with co-authors from different countries.


Practical aims:

  • Learn to improve clarity and usage in English.
  • Strengthen your argumentation.
  • Avoid common pitfalls in English usage, grammar, syntax.
  • Avoid ambiguity, repetition, inadvertent plagiarism.
  • Learn how to plan effectively and structure efficiently a paper for publication.
  • Learn how to analyse, interpret, and synthesise new material for publication. 
  • Learn how to write effective proposals in English for projects and papers.
  • Address productively the concerns and suggestions of peer reviewers.
